13 Orientations of matroids

13.1 Find orientations of a given geometric lattice

13.1-1 LFindOrientations
‣ LFindOrientations( L )( operation )

Returns: A list of oriented matroids

Computes all oriented matroids (up to some isomorphisms) which have L as underlying matroid structe or geometric lattice.

Depending on this size of L this might take a considerable amount of time and there may be many orientations depending on the sparsety of relations in L. The algorithm reformulates the problem of determining orientations to an SAT-instance and then uses an SAT-solver (currently PicoSAT as a standard) to solve.

gap> A:=AGpql(2,2,3); L:=IntersectionLattice(A); Bs:=LBases(L);; Length(Bs);
<HyperplaneArrangement: 6 hyperplanes in 3-space>
<Geometric lattice: 6 atoms, rank 3>
16
gap> OMs:=FindOrientations(L);
